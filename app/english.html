Story

Hi, my name is Jacqueline Unanue, I am a Chilean-Spanish-American visual artist, living and working in art for almost fifteen years in Philadelphia, PA, United States.

My Project and where:

I have been invited to exhibit individually during 2015, in three prestigious galleries, trough three countries.

I would like to invite you to feel part of my project about this traveling exhibition of my series titled:My Ancient Land.The first one, will be held at Chilean Embassy in Washington DC, United States, during March; the second one at Sala Viña del Mar, in Viña del Mar city, Chile, from April 14 to May 9; and the third one, at Casa Elizalde, in Barcelona, Spain, from September 15 to October 16.

My Ancient Land, “Sextet I” | 2014 | acrylic on canvas | 58”x 69” (147x 175 cm) Inspired by composer Juan Orrego-Salas * In traveling exhibition


My Ancient Land series is related to my most recent abstract paintings inspired by the renowned Chilean classical composers: Juan Orrego-Salas: “Sextet for B flat clarinet, string quartet & piano, Op. 38, movement 1”; Enrique Soro: “Fantastic Dance”; Jorge Urrutia: “Suggestions of Chile Op.1”; Eulogio Dávalos: "Cueca for Pablo Neruda" and "Siempre"; Sylvia Soublet: “Roman Mass”, and René Amengual, “Symphonic Prelude”,

My Ancient Land, “Sextet II” | 2014 | acrylic on canvas | 30”x 40” (76x 102cm) Inspired by composer Juan Orrego-Salas * In traveling exhibition


Immersed in this music as I painted, l felt it flow through me physically and found myself expressing both the movement of the music and my own emotions in free and spontaneous brush strokes that are musical as well as gestural.

Chile, the country where I was born and where I developed my artistic career, is firmly recorded in my memory. The colors I have used suggest -in an abstract way-, my memories of the geography, the forest, the desert, the ocean, and the Chilean sky.

Those memories are meshed with both the experiences I had in my many travels to Spain to connect with my father’s ancestral roots and also with my experience in the United States where I have lived for almost fifteen years and where I continue to develop an artistic vision formed by my multi-cultural experiences.

My Ancient Land, “Suggestions of Chile II” | 2014 | acrylic on canvas | 11”x 16.5” (28x 42cm) Inspired by composer Jorge Urrutia * In traveling exhibition

My Ancient Land, “Roman Mass I” | 2014 | acrylic on canvas | 30”x 40” (76x 102cm) Inspired by composer Sylvia Soublette * In traveling exhibition

The resultant paintings –some of them of big dimensions- serve as coded maps, leading the viewer to the Illo Tempore where, over time, we are part of a universal root system without cultural, territorial, or continental distinctions. 

This journey toward universal origins intrigues me, and it spontaneously appears in my work.

About the artist

Jacqueline Unanue was born in Chile, and studied design at the Universidad de Chile de Valparaíso. At that time she became interested in pre-Columbian art, especially the rock art done in her native country. She met archaeologist Hans Niemeyer, the most important scholar of Chilean rock art, who became her mentor.

Together with her husband Ricardo Guajardo, who is also a designer and graphic artist, Unanue traveled extensively through Chile’s Atacama Desert, doing on-site research in the mountains and cliff areas that contain art that was carved or painted thousands of years ago. She also traveled to Spain to study the pre-historic paintings of the Altamira caves in the Basque Country, which being the home of her paternal ancestors connected her to her roots.

She won the Critics Award in Visual Arts by the Association of Art Critics, Valparaiso, Chile. Twice she has obtained grant funds DIRAC, Department of Cultural Affairs, Ministry of Foreign Affairs of Chile for her exhibitions in Guayasamin Foundation, Quito, Ecuador, in 1996 and the Inter-American Development Bank Gallery in Washington DC, in 2000.

Obtuvo el Premio de la Crítica en Artes Visuales de la Asociación de Críticos de Arte de Valparaíso en Chile. Dos veces ha recibido el auspicio de DIRAC Direción de Asuntos Culturales, del Ministerio de Relaciones Exteriores, para sus exposiones en la Fundación Guayasamín, Quito, Ecuador, en 1996 y en el BID, Banco Interamericano de Desarrollo, en Washington DC, en el  2000.

In addition to numerous individual and group exhibitions in Chile since 1983, she has also been exhibited in Spain, Finland, Ecuador, Argentina, and the United States—in galleries in Washington D.C., Philadelphia, Chicago, and New York.

Since her arrival in the United States in 2000, her artistic vision has mainly been expressed through abstract acrylic painting on canvas. She is represented by the 3rd Street Gallery, Philadelphia, PA, USA: http://www.3rdstreetgallery.com/
